# 20-Minute Presentation Outline

Use this structure for the class presentation.

## 1. Opening: The Problem

Explain the problem in simple terms:

- students often feel stress before exams
- they struggle to choose what to study first
- existing tools are too generic or too manual

## 2. Target Audience

Describe:

- school students
- university students
- users with time-management and exam-stress difficulties

## 3. User Research

Present:

- how many users you interviewed or observed
- major recurring pain points
- 2 to 3 important insights

## 4. Design Challenge

Present the final challenge statement:

How might we help students create realistic and motivating study plans that reduce stress and improve consistency?

## 5. Ideation

Explain:

- what ideas were considered
- why the AI-powered planner was chosen

## 6. The Solution

Present AI Study Planner as:

- a mobile-first study planning assistant
- not a generic task manager
- a product that combines planning, prioritization, and support

## 7. Demo Flow

Recommended live demo order:

1. Register or login
2. Onboarding
3. Add subjects
4. Generate AI plan
5. Show My Plan
6. Show Progress
7. Ask AI Coach a question

## 8. AI Integration

Explain clearly:

- what data the AI uses
- how the plan is personalized
- why this is more meaningful than a normal planner

## 9. UX Strengths

Highlight:

- one main action per screen
- simple forms
- calm visual style
- clear navigation
- motivating but practical language

## 10. Limits and Future Development

Mention:

- this is a prototype
- more user validation is needed
- future plans such as calendar sync, reminders, and adaptive planning

## 11. Closing

End with:

- the value for students
- the innovation in combining UX and AI
- how the app helps transform stress into structured action
