# AI Study Planner: Course Alignment

This file maps the project directly to the final project requirements in the Design Thinking 2 brief.

## 1. Problem Definition

AI Study Planner addresses a real user problem:

- high school and university students often struggle to organize study time
- students feel stress before exams because they do not know what to prioritize
- many students underestimate how much time difficult subjects require
- generic to-do tools do not adapt planning to academic deadlines, difficulty, or realistic daily availability

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- high school students preparing for exams and assignments
- university students balancing several subjects with different difficulty levels

Typical user needs:

- clear priorities
- realistic daily study tasks
- lower stress
- better time management
- guidance that feels personal, not generic

## 3. User Research Structure

The course brief asks for user-needs research such as observations, short interviews, or focus groups.

This project pack includes a template for:

- short interview summaries
- observation notes
- pain points and recurring themes

Important:

- do not invent research results
- replace all placeholders with real findings from your own interviews or observations

## 4. Design Challenge

Suggested design challenge statement:

How might we help school and university students build calm, realistic, personalized study plans that reduce stress and improve consistency before exams and deadlines?

## 5. Ideation

Suggested ideas to describe in the report:

- a simple manual planner
- a reminder-based study assistant
- a visual calendar for exam preparation
- an AI-powered planner that uses deadlines, difficulty, priority, and daily available hours

Chosen idea:

- the AI-powered study planning assistant

Why this idea was chosen:

- it solves a real planning problem, not just task logging
- it uses AI in a meaningful way
- it creates a stronger innovation story for the course project

## 6. Prototype Quality

The prototype currently includes:

- register and login
- onboarding
- home dashboard
- subjects list and subject form
- AI plan generation
- my plan screen
- progress screen
- profile screen
- AI Coach chat

This supports the course requirement for a realistic interface prototype and a clear demo flow.

## 7. AI Integration

The AI component is meaningful because it does more than answer open chat questions.

It uses:

- academic level
- preferred language
- study goal
- daily available hours
- subject list
- exam dates
- difficulty
- priority
- notes

It produces:

- AI summary
- weekly study plan
- today's tasks
- study tips
- motivation support
- AI Coach chat assistance

## 8. UX Strengths

Key UX strengths to mention:

- mobile-first structure
- one main action per screen
- simple forms
- clear bottom navigation
- calm visual design
- progress visibility
- actionable feedback

## 9. Innovation Value

The innovative value of the app is not only that it uses AI, but that it uses AI to make planning more realistic and emotionally supportive for students.

Instead of acting like a generic task manager, the app acts like a study planning assistant.

## 10. Limits and Future Development

Suggested limitations section:

- the prototype still depends on user-provided subject details
- research findings must be validated with real student interviews
- the current version focuses on text planning rather than calendar sync or notifications

Suggested future development:

- calendar integration
- push reminders
- shared study groups
- adaptive plans based on actual completion history
- exam-period intensity modes
